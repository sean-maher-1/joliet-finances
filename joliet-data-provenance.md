## Headline figures (2024)
- Net financial position: **−$1.24B** (≈ −$24,300 per household; 51,055 households, ACS 2024)
- Accumulated depreciation: $711.6M → deferred repair bill (×1.75–2.25) ≈ **$1.25B–$1.6B** (≈ $24,400–$31,400/household)
- Combined (on-books position + off-books repair bill): ≈ **−$2.7B** (≈ −$52,000/household)
- The −$1.24B hole is driven by: retiree health care/OPEB (~$500M liability), net pension liability (~$389M),
  and debt (~$447M, mostly existing water/sewer loans and GO bonds; the Lake Michigan program is largely future borrowing). Two accounting reforms made the promises
  visible: GASB 68 (pensions, 2015) and GASB 75 (OPEB, 2018).
